# Template Architecture

Templates are the reusable structural units of the Engine Room.  
They ensure consistency, reduce cognitive load, and make complex tasks repeatable across projects, platforms, and AI agents.

## Purpose of Template Architecture
Templates exist to:
- standardize recurring tasks  
- accelerate execution  
- reduce decision fatigue  
- maintain system-wide consistency  
- support multi-agent AI collaboration  

A template is not a script — it is a structured starting point.

## Template Structure
All templates follow a consistent internal architecture:

1. **Purpose**  
   A short statement describing what the template is for and when it should be used.

2. **Inputs**  
   The information, assets, or context required to use the template effectively.

3. **Sections**  
   The core structural components of the template.  
   Each section is clearly labeled and contains placeholders or prompts.

4. **Usage Notes**  
   Guidance on how to apply the template, including variations or edge cases.

5. **Version Notes**  
   Any updates or refinements made over time.

## Template Design Principles
All templates must align with:

- **Clarity** — sections are explicit and easy to follow  
- **Modularity** — templates can be combined or nested  
- **AI-Native Structure** — templates assume collaboration with AI agents  
- **Scalability** — templates should work across multiple contexts  
- **Consistency** — naming, formatting, and structure follow system standards  

## Template Categories
Templates fall into several categories:

- **Creative Templates** — scripts, outlines, narrative structures  
- **Operational Templates** — checklists, publishing formats, documentation  
- **Analytical Templates** — research structures, comparison matrices  
- **Portfolio Templates** — case studies, project summaries, content blocks  
- **System Templates** — multi-agent orchestration patterns  

Each category has its own subfolder and naming conventions.

## Living Template System
Templates evolve as workflows mature and new needs emerge.  
Old templates are versioned, not overwritten, to preserve system history.

The template architecture ensures that every new template strengthens the system rather than adding noise.
