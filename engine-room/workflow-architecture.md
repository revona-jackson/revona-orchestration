# Workflow Architecture

Workflows are the backbone of the Engine Room.  
They define how tasks move from intention to completion in a clear, repeatable, AI‑native sequence.  
This page outlines the structural rules that all workflows follow.

## Purpose of Workflow Architecture
The goal of workflow architecture is to:
- reduce decision fatigue  
- create predictable execution patterns  
- enable multi‑agent AI collaboration  
- ensure every task has a clear beginning, middle, and end  

Workflows turn creative and operational chaos into structured momentum.

## Standard Workflow Structure
All workflows follow a consistent structure:

1. **Trigger**  
   What starts the workflow — an idea, a task, a need, or a scheduled cycle.

2. **Inputs**  
   What the workflow requires to run — files, prompts, context, tools, or decisions.

3. **Process Steps**  
   A clear, ordered sequence of actions.  
   Each step is atomic, actionable, and AI‑collaborative.

4. **Outputs**  
   What the workflow produces — assets, drafts, decisions, documentation, or updates.

5. **Completion Criteria**  
   How you know the workflow is done.  
   No ambiguity, no loose ends.

6. **Version Notes**  
   Any changes, improvements, or refinements made over time.

## Workflow Design Principles
All workflows must align with:

- **Clarity** — steps are explicit and unambiguous  
- **Reusability** — workflows can be applied repeatedly with minimal modification  
- **AI‑Native Thinking** — steps assume collaboration with multiple AI agents  
- **Scalability** — workflows should grow with the system  
- **Traceability** — decisions and outputs are documented  

## Workflow Categories
Workflows fall into several categories:

- **Creative Workflows** — writing, ambience creation, video production  
- **Operational Workflows** — publishing, scheduling, asset management  
- **Analytical Workflows** — research, synthesis, optimization  
- **Maintenance Workflows** — updates, audits, refinements  
- **Portfolio Workflows** — content creation, case studies, cross‑platform expansion  

Each category has its own templates and standards.

## Living Architecture
Workflows evolve as tools improve, AI agents expand, and creative processes mature.  
This page defines the structure — the workflows themselves live throughout the Engine Room.
