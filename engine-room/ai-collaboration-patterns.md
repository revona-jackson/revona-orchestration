# AI Collaboration Patterns

AI Collaboration Patterns define how humans and AI agents work together across workflows and systems.  
These patterns ensure clarity, consistency, and efficiency in multi-agent orchestration.

## Purpose of AI Collaboration Patterns
These patterns exist to:
- standardize how AI participates in workflows  
- reduce friction in human–AI handoffs  
- enable parallel agent collaboration  
- maintain context across tools and tasks  
- ensure predictable, reliable execution  

AI collaboration is not an afterthought — it is a core design principle.

## Collaboration Modes

### **1. AI-Led Mode**
The AI agent drives the process.  
The human provides:
- initial context  
- constraints  
- final approval  

Used for:
- drafting  
- research  
- synthesis  
- optimization  

### **2. Human-Led Mode**
The human drives the process.  
The AI provides:
- support  
- suggestions  
- execution assistance  

Used for:
- creative direction  
- strategic decisions  
- subjective judgment  

### **3. Co-Creation Mode**
Human and AI alternate leading roles.  
Handoffs are intentional and documented.

Used for:
- writing  
- design  
- ideation  
- narrative development  

### **4. Parallel Agent Mode**
Multiple AI agents work simultaneously on different tasks.  
A coordinator (human or AI) merges outputs.

Used for:
- research  
- analysis  
- multi-part content creation  

### **5. Orchestration Mode**
A system coordinates multiple agents, workflows, and tools.  
The human oversees the system rather than individual tasks.

Used for:
- complex systems  
- multi-stage pipelines  
- large creative projects  

## Handoff Standards
All handoffs must include:
- current state  
- next action  
- constraints  
- success criteria  
- known risks or blockers  

Handoffs are explicit, not implied.

## Context Management
AI agents must always receive:
- relevant background  
- current task state  
- boundaries  
- desired output format  
- examples (if helpful)  

Context is the fuel of collaboration.

## Error Handling Patterns
When an AI agent encounters an issue:
1. Identify the failure mode  
2. Provide a brief explanation  
3. Suggest recovery options  
4. Escalate to human if needed  

Errors are part of the system, not a disruption.

## Collaboration Principles
All AI collaboration follows these principles:

- **Transparency** — AI explains reasoning when relevant  
- **Alignment** — AI follows system principles and boundaries  
- **Efficiency** — minimize unnecessary back-and-forth  
- **Traceability** — decisions and outputs are documented  
- **Scalability** — patterns support multi-agent expansion  

## Living Collaboration Framework
AI Collaboration Patterns evolve as tools improve and new agents join the system.  
Updates are versioned and documented to maintain clarity and continuity.
