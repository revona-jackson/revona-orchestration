# Quality Standards

Quality Standards define the criteria that all workflows, systems, templates, and outputs must meet before they are considered complete.  
These standards ensure consistency, clarity, and excellence across the entire Revona orchestration system.

## Purpose of Quality Standards
Quality Standards exist to:
- maintain a high level of creative and operational excellence  
- ensure outputs align with system philosophy and design language  
- prevent drift as the system scales  
- support reliable AI collaboration  
- create confidence in every published or archived asset  

Quality is not subjective — it is defined.

## Core Quality Criteria

### **1. Clarity**
All outputs must be:
- easy to understand  
- free of ambiguity  
- structured logically  
- aligned with system terminology  

If something requires explanation, it is not yet clear.

### **2. Consistency**
Outputs must follow:
- naming conventions  
- formatting standards  
- workflow and template structures  
- design language principles  

Consistency builds trust in the system.

### **3. Completeness**
An output is only complete when:
- all required sections are filled  
- all dependencies are satisfied  
- all steps have been executed  
- version notes are updated  
- assets are stored correctly  

No partial or “almost done” states.

### **4. Alignment**
Outputs must align with:
- system principles  
- creative discipline framework  
- decision framework  
- system boundaries  

If an output violates a principle, it must be revised.

### **5. Craft**
Creative outputs must demonstrate:
- intentionality  
- atmosphere  
- narrative cohesion  
- aesthetic coherence  

Craft is the signature of the studio.

## Quality Gates
Before an output is approved, it must pass through quality gates:

1. **Structural Gate**  
   Does it follow the correct template or workflow?

2. **Content Gate**  
   Is the content complete, accurate, and aligned?

3. **Design Gate**  
   Does it follow the design language?

4. **Technical Gate**  
   Are assets named, stored, and versioned correctly?

5. **Final Gate**  
   Does it meet the creative or operational intent?

Only after passing all gates is an output considered final.

## AI Collaboration Considerations
AI-generated outputs must:
- follow system standards  
- include clear reasoning when relevant  
- be reviewed for alignment and clarity  
- be revised when necessary  

AI is a collaborator, not an autopilot.

## Living Quality Framework
Quality Standards evolve as the studio matures.  
Updates are documented and versioned to maintain clarity and historical continuity.

This page ensures that every output — creative, operational, analytical, or systemic — meets the same high standard of excellence.
