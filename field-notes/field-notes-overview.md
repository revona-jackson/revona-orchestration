# Field Notes Overview

Field Notes is the reflective pillar of the Revona orchestration system.  
Where Blueprint defines identity and Engine Room defines execution, Field Notes captures the lived experience of using the system.

This pillar contains observations, experiments, insights, iteration logs, and creative reflections that inform future improvements.

## Purpose of Field Notes
Field Notes exists to:
- document insights gained during real-world execution  
- capture experiments, prototypes, and tests  
- record observations that refine workflows and systems  
- support continuous improvement  
- preserve creative and operational intuition  

Field Notes is the memory of the studio.

## What Lives Here
The Field Notes pillar contains:
- **Experiment Logs** — tests, prototypes, trials  
- **Observational Notes** — insights from using workflows and systems  
- **Iteration Logs** — what changed, why, and what was learned  
- **Creative Reflections** — thoughts on process, craft, and direction  
- **Research Notes** — findings that inform future work  

## Relationship to Other Pillars
- Blueprint defines the philosophy  
- Engine Room defines the execution  
- **Field Notes captures the evolution**

This pillar ensures the system grows with intention and awareness.

## Living Reflective System
Field Notes is updated continuously as insights emerge.  
It is the most fluid and exploratory pillar in the system.
