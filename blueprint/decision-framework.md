# Decision Framework

The Revona orchestration system relies on clear, repeatable decision‑making patterns. This framework defines how choices are evaluated, documented, and aligned with the system’s philosophy.

## Principles‑Aligned Decisions
Every decision is checked against the core principles of the system.  
If a choice violates a principle, the choice is reconsidered.

## Context Before Action
Decisions are made only after gathering the relevant context — goals, constraints, dependencies, and downstream effects.

## Documented Rationale
Every significant decision includes a brief explanation of *why* it was made.  
This creates traceability and prevents future confusion.

## Reversibility Awareness
The system distinguishes between reversible and irreversible decisions.  
Reversible decisions are made quickly; irreversible ones are made carefully.

## Bias‑Reduction Patterns
AI and human biases are acknowledged and mitigated through structured evaluation, comparison, and reflection.

## Decision as a System Artifact
Decisions are treated as part of the architecture — not ephemeral moments.  
They shape the system and must be recorded accordingly.
