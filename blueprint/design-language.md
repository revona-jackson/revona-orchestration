# Design Language

The Revona orchestration system uses a consistent design language to maintain clarity, coherence, and emotional tone across all documentation and workflows. This language shapes how information is presented and how the system feels to use.

## Calm Structure
Pages are clean, spacious, and intentional.  
Whitespace is used as a design tool, not an afterthought.

## Hierarchical Clarity
Headings, subheadings, and sections follow predictable patterns.  
Readers always know where they are within the structure.

## Voice and Tone
The system speaks with clarity, confidence, and warmth.  
It avoids jargon unless necessary and explains concepts with precision.

## Visual Simplicity
The design avoids clutter, decoration, or unnecessary visual noise.  
Simplicity is treated as a form of respect for the reader.

## Semantic Consistency
Terms, labels, and concepts are used consistently across all pages.  
A term defined once becomes a stable part of the system’s vocabulary.

## Documentation as Experience
The design language treats documentation as a guided experience — not just text on a page.  
The goal is to make the system feel intuitive, elegant, and alive.
